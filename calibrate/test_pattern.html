Calibrate(TM) Display Verification

NeuralUX(TM) Calibration Test Patterns

1. Grayscale Ramp (0-255)

What to look for:

  • Smooth transitions with no banding or steps
  • All 32 steps should be distinguishable
  • No color tint in the grays (should be neutral)

2. Gamma Verification (Target: 2.2-2.4)

Solid 47%

Should match checkerboard

50% Checkerboard

Solid 50%

Reference mid-gray

Gamma Test:

  • At gamma 2.2, the solid 47% gray should match the checkerboard brightness
  • If the checkerboard looks brighter, gamma is too high
  • If the checkerboard looks darker, gamma is too low

3. Color Bars (100% Saturation)

What to look for:

  • Colors should be vivid and saturated
  • No color bleeding between bars
  • White should be neutral (no pink/green/blue tint)

5. White Point Test (D65 = 6500K)

Warm (5000K)
D65 (6500K)
Cool (7500K)

White Point Check:

  • The center patch should appear as pure white
  • Left patch should look slightly warm/yellow
  • Right patch should look slightly cool/blue
  • If center looks tinted, white point may be off

Shadow Detail (OLED Important):

  • On OLED: Level 0 should be pure black (pixel off)
  • You should be able to distinguish levels 3-5 and above
  • If all look the same, shadow detail is crushed
